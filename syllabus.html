Fall 2008

Course Overview

The purpose of this course is to introduce the R and Python programming languages as computing tools of data science. The first half of the course will focus on R, the second half will focus on Python. This class is for the Master of Financial Mathematics program, therefore all in-class examples and homework assignments will involve financial data.

I will teach the course assuming that that students have no programming background. However, I am in no way attempting to be a comprehensive introduction to programming. Rather, I have amasses a set of analyses (or analysis-related exercises) I want all the student to complete, and I am simply going to introduce any programming concepts that will be needed.

I have designed the course so that students with strong programming skills will also find the material valuable. Students who need to spend less time working on the foundational programming skill, can spend more time digging into the rich financial data sets that we will have at our disposal.

Materials

Students are expected to have a computer capable of running the latest versions of R, R Studio, and the Anaconda distribution. All this software is free. I will assign a variety of reading throughout the semester - all of it will be freely available online.

Homework

This is a pass/fail course, so I am going to assign homeworks largely for your benefit - so that you can get practice and feedback. Homework is a product that you are paying for, and grading is a service that you are paying for. I’m going to keep my end of the bargain, but you have to keep your end. In that vein, late homework assignments will not be accepted.

The T.A. and I are going provide these, respectively.

Homework will be turned in through Github. This is for convenience, and is also a way to force you to learn about version control.

Grading

This is an elective pass/fail course. As such, I am not particularly concerned about assessing your skills. Rather, I get to focus on providing you with valuable knowledge and skills. But I will give you problems to work on, and to to turn in, as the feedback you get from the graders will be useful to you.

There will be two projects at the end of each section, one on R and one on Python.

Weekly Course Plan

Part 1: R & RStudio

Week 1 - Preliminaries

We’ll do the usual first class housekeeping including walking though the syllabus. Then, we’ll make sure that everyone has R & R Studio installed. And then there is going to be a smattering of introductory programming material: files, directories, paths, vectore, dataframes, the tidyverse, reading in data from a CSV, subsetting a dataframe, basic dplyr functionality. We’ll end the class by introducing options contracts.

Assignment:

  • Read chapter 5 of R4DS - you should type out all the examples along with him.

  • Open a GitHub account.

  • Complete the Software Carpentry Lesson on Git and GitHub

Week 2 - Data Manipulation

This week will be focused on gaining proficiency the data manipulation functions of dplyr with: filter(), arrange(), select(), mutate(), groupby(), summarize(). We will then put all these together the pipe operator. We will also discuss more about options.

Week 3 - Functions and Iteration

You now have a bit of experience with writing scripts. The next level of sophistication is writing functions. We will go through a variety of functions related to data: cleaning a dataframe, an option payoff function. We will also talk about iterating with for-loops, iterating with the tidyverse purrr() package.

Week 4 - DataViz 1

Data Visualization with ggplot2(). An introduction to some of the most popular finance related packages on CRAN. Discuss how to explore packages on your own and other self-study resources.

Week 5 - DataViz 2 & Project Assignment More data visualization and the R project with be assigned.
